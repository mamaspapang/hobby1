Types of Gardening

1. Container Gardening: This type of gardening involves growing plants in pots or containers. It's perfect for those with limited space or who want to bring greenery indoors. Container gardening offers versatility and allows you to easily move plants around to find the ideal growing conditions.

2. Raised Bed Gardening: In raised bed gardening, plants are grown in beds that are elevated from the ground. This method provides better drainage, prevents soil compaction, and offers improved control over the soil quality. Q: How often should I water my garden?

A: The watering frequency depends on various factors such as the type of plants, weather conditions, and soil moisture. Remember to water your garden deeply, ensuring that the water reaches the roots. As a general rule, most gardens require about 1-2 inches of water per week.

Q: How do I prevent pests in my garden?

A: Pest prevention can be achieved through various methods, including using organic pesticides, practicing companion planting, and maintaining proper garden hygiene. Regularly inspect your plants for any signs of pests or diseases, and take prompt action to prevent their spread.
