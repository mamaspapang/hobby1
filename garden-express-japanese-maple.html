The Garden's History

The Portland Japanese Garden has a rich history dating back to its establishment in 1963. It was designed by famous Japanese garden designer Takuma Tono, who sought to create a space that would promote peace, tranquility, and harmony.

The garden covers an area of 12 acres and is divided into several distinct sections, each showcasing a different aspect of traditional Japanese gardening. From the serene Strolling Pond Garden to the vibrant Tea Garden, there's something for everyone to enjoy.

Immerse Yourself in Nature

One of the highlights of visiting the Portland Japanese Garden is the opportunity to immerse yourself in nature. As you explore the garden, you'll come across beautiful maple trees, vibrant cherry blossoms, and meticulously manicured bonsai trees.

Japanese Tea House Experience

No visit to the Portland Japanese Garden is complete without experiencing a traditional Japanese tea ceremony. The garden features a charming Tea House where you can partake in this ancient ritual.

Step inside and be transported to a different time and place. Sip on a cup of matcha tea while enjoying the serene views of the garden. It's an experience that will leave you feeling calm, centered, and connected to Japanese culture.

People Also Ask

Q: How much does it cost to visit the Portland Japanese Garden?

A: The admission fee for adults is $18.95, and discounts are available for seniors, students, and children.

Q: Are there guided tours available at the garden?

A: Yes, guided tours are available at an additional cost. They provide in-depth knowledge about the garden's history, design, and cultural significance.

Q: Can I take photographs in the Portland Japanese Garden?

A: Yes, photography is allowed in most areas of the garden. However, tripods and drones are not permitted without prior permission.
