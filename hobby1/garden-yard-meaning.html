Hey there! Today, let's talk about the difference between lawn, yard, turf, and garden. Whether you're an aspiring green thumb or simply curious about the terminology, this post will clear things up for you.

When we say "lawn," we're referring to a specific area covered in grass that is usually mowed and kept well-manicured. It's commonly found outside homes, parks, and other public spaces. The keyword here is "lawn."

A yard, on the other hand, encompasses the entire outdoor area around a house or building. It includes everything from the front yard to the backyard, and it can contain various elements like grass, trees, plants, and even structures like patios or swimming pools. So, the yard is a broader term compared to a lawn.
