Add Some Color

Now that your flower beds are looking fresh and tidy, it's time to add some color. May is the perfect time to plant annuals and perennials that will bloom throughout the summer months. Consider adding some vibrant petunias, cheerful marigolds, or fragrant lavender to your garden. Not only will they add a pop of color, but they'll also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Caring for Your Lawn

While you're working in the garden, don't forget to give some attention to your lawn. May is a great time to give it some much-needed love and care. Start by mowing the grass on a higher setting, as longer grass helps to shade the roots and prevent weeds from taking hold. If you notice any bare patches or thinning areas, consider overseeding to encourage new growth. And of course, don't forget to water regularly to keep your lawn looking lush and green.

Q: What other tasks are important to do in May?

Q: How often should I water my plants in May?

A: It's important to keep an eye on the weather and adjust your watering schedule accordingly. Generally, plants require more water in warmer weather, so you may need to water them every 2-3 days. However, be sure to check the moisture level of the soil before watering to avoid over-watering.

Q: Can I still prune my plants in May?

A: While pruning is typically done in the dormant season, there are some exceptions. May is a good time to prune spring-flowering shrubs and trees after they have finished blooming. However, avoid pruning during hot and dry periods to prevent stress on the plants.
